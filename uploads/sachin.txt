Sachin, often referred to as the "Little Master" or "Master Blaster," is a former Indian international cricketer and one of the greatest batsmen in the history of cricket. Born on April 24, 1973, in Mumbai, India, Sachin is widely regarded as one of the most complete batsmen of all time, with a prolific career spanning over two decades.

Sachin made his international debut for India at the age of 16 in a Test match against Pakistan in November 1989. He quickly rose to prominence with his exceptional talent and became the youngest player to score a century in international cricket at the age of 17. Throughout his illustrious career, Sachin amassed numerous records and achievements in both Test cricket and One Day Internationals (ODIs).

During his international career, Sachin represented India in 200 Test matches and 463 ODIs, scoring a total of 15,921 runs in Tests and 18,426 runs in ODIs. He holds the record for the most runs scored in both Test and ODI cricket. Sachin's consistency and ability to adapt to different formats of the game made him a formidable batsman in all conditions.

One of Sachin's most remarkable achievements is scoring 100 international centuries, a milestone that reflects his incredible skill, determination, and longevity in the sport. This feat includes 51 centuries in Test cricket and 49 centuries in ODIs, making him the first and only player to achieve this milestone in the history of cricket.

Sachin played for several domestic and international teams throughout his career, including the Mumbai cricket team in Indian domestic cricket and various franchise teams in Twenty20 leagues such as the Indian Premier League (IPL). He was a key member of the Mumbai Indians franchise in the IPL, serving as both a player and a mentor, and played a significant role in the team's success in the tournament.

Sachin is known for his humility, sportsmanship, and philanthropic efforts. He has been actively involved in charitable activities and initiatives aimed at promoting education, healthcare, and sports development in India. Sachin's contributions to society earned him numerous awards and accolades, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, in 2014.

After retiring from international cricket in 2013, Sachin continues to be involved in various cricket-related activities, including mentoring young cricketers and promoting the sport at the grassroots level. His legacy as a cricketing legend and a role model for aspiring cricketers will continue to inspire future generations for years to come.
